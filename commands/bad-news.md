Help craft a message that delivers bad news using the BIFF framework (Brief, Informative, Friendly, Firm).

The situation: $ARGUMENTS

First, think through this step by step before writing anything:

1. Identify what the bad news actually is and gauge severity (minor inconvenience vs. major impact).
2. Identify the likely audience and relationship (boss, client, team, stakeholder).
3. Determine what, if anything, can be offered as a realistic path forward -- not a fake consolation, but a genuine next step.
4. Draft three versions using the BIFF principles:
   - **Brief**: Don't bury the news in a wall of text.
   - **Informative**: Include what happened and what comes next.
   - **Friendly**: Acknowledge the impact without groveling.
   - **Firm**: Don't leave room for negotiation on facts that aren't negotiable.

Produce exactly three versions at different directness levels so the user can pick the one that fits:

---

### Version 1: Soft Landing
Maximum cushioning while still being honest. Good for sensitive situations or when the relationship is more important than speed.

### Version 2: Straight Shooter
Clear, direct, no extra padding. The default for most professional situations.

### Version 3: Band-Aid Rip
Minimal words, maximum clarity. For when everyone already knows it's coming and just needs confirmation.

---

Before outputting, self-check against these criteria:
- Does each version actually deliver the bad news clearly?
- Would the recipient know the news after the first two sentences?
- Are the three versions genuinely different in directness, not just different in length?
- Is the path forward realistic, not just a platitude?
- Did you avoid blame-shifting language?

Anti-patterns to avoid:
- Do NOT bury the bad news after three paragraphs of setup.
- Do NOT use "unfortunately" more than once across all three versions combined.
- Do NOT offer fake optimism ("this is actually a great opportunity").
- Do NOT blame other teams or individuals unless the user specifically asks for that framing.
- Do NOT use the phrases "at this time" or "going forward" or "moving forward."
- Do NOT pad with unnecessary apologies.
- Do NOT use em dashes anywhere. Use -- or commas or periods instead.
